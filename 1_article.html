The Depression Diaries

This is first chapter

At dawn, the old lighthouse blinked awake. Inside, a retired sailor brewed tea, listening to waves argue with rocks. He wrote letters he never sent, folded them into paper boats, and released them to the sea—hoping the tide still remembered his name.

That night, the boats returned, soaked but intact, circling the shore. Each carried a reply in unfamiliar ink—coordinates, stars, a promise. The sailor smiled for the first time in years, packed his compass, and left the light on for whoever was coming home.
